Rise, set and visibility from different latitudes

The comet rises and sets at different times depending on the observer's latitude and longitude. The plots below provide information for approximating when the comet will be visible, and how long it will be observable from a given location on a given date.

Plots are given for several major observatories, covering a range of latitudes that are generally representative of most observers. Rise and set times are given in UTC for 0° longitude. For other latitudes, these times will be approximate to the local time, which should again be sufficient for basic planning purposes. Note, however, that there will be an offset (typically < 1/2 hr) for different longitudes, so if more accuracy is needed, then you should obtain a more detailed ephemeris.
